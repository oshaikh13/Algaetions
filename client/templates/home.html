Deforestation

Deforestation is clearing Earth's forests on a massive scale, often resulting in damage to the quality of the land and the ecosystem around it.

Photosynthesis

The process in which plants take in Carbon Dioxide and Water, and yeild Oxygen and Glucose.

Photosynthesis can be summarized in a simple chemical equation

6CO2 + 6H2O = C6H12O6 + 6O2

Carbon Sinks

A carbon sink is anything that stores more carbon than it releases, lowering overall CO2 rates in the atmosphere.

Loss of Forests

Forests are a common example of carbon sinks. They store exess carbon in the atmosphere, and release it at a rapid rate when cut down. Deforestation has removed various carbon sinks in the world.

MMR

Myanmar lost 7,445,000 hectares of forest cover between 1990 and 2010.

Ranked #3 in deforestation rates (UN).

THA

Thailand lost 43 percent of its forests between 1973 and 2009.

Main causes include illegal logging.

BGD

Only 6.7 percent of Bangladesh has forest cover

2,000 hectares of forest cover are lost per year

Bengal Bay

The worlds largest bay. Bordered by India, Bangladesh, Myanmar, and Thailand.

The bay was known as a "strong carbon sink" in 1991. It stored excess CO2 in the atmosphere. In 2011, the CO2 rates were considerably higher than those in 1991. This may have been a cause of deforestation.

Why?

Deforestation removed lots of carbon sinks near Bengal Bay. Excess carbon would now go to either of two places: The atmosphere, or the sea. Too much of either option is bad. The atmopshere holding excess CO2 causes global warming. The sea, however, takes in the excess carbon and increases its concentration in the water.

Algae Blooms

CO2, for some cases of photosynthesis, is known as a limiting factor for the reaction. This means that certain species of plants are unable to grow because of the lack of CO2 in the ecosystem.

Because there is no longer a lack of CO2 in aquatic ecosystems, CO2 is no longer a limiting factor, increasing the amount of algae blooms in the sea.

Prevention

Deforestation is a significant issue that plagues several countries around the world. Its various effects include the rise of carbon dioxide levels in the sea, causing a potential increase in algae blooms.

However, organizations around the world are raising awareness, or are attempting to prevent the aggravation of this issue.
